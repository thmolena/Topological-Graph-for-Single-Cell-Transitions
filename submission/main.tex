\title{Directed spectral-truncated graph operators for inductive forecasting of single-cell transitions}

\author{Molena Huynh}
\email{molena.huynh@jmp.com}
\affiliation{North Carolina State University, Raleigh, North Carolina 27695, USA}
\date{\today}

\begin{abstract}
Despite the centrality of the cell $k$-nearest-neighbor ($k$NN) graph to single-cell analysis, the
operator used to propagate information across it---the self-adjoint normalized adjacency
$S=D^{-1/2}AD^{-1/2}$---is poorly matched to forecasting cell-state transitions. It is reversible, so it
cannot encode the arrow of differentiation that pseudotime makes explicit; and when the graph is built
over all cells it consults the held-out connectivity at inference, so a measured gain over a point
classifier conflates the value of diffusion with a transductive information advantage. We resolve both
limitations with a single object: a spectral-truncated, pseudotime-directed graph operator. Each edge is
reweighted by a bounded forward kernel of the pseudotime increment and row-normalized, yielding a
non-self-adjoint propagator that transports labels along the trajectory; the diffusion is then restricted
to the leading $r$ graph-Fourier modes---a band-limiting spectral truncation that denoises the operator
and admits a closed-form solve---and an out-of-sample Nystr\"om extension makes the forecaster inductive,
so held-out connectivity is never used. On a seeded synthetic branching lineage ($n=\num{3000}$ cells,
$5$ seeds) the inductive operator improves held-out accuracy over a matched inductive $k$NN classifier on
all four leakage-checked transfer protocols ($\num{0.6856}$ versus $\num{0.5992}$) and recovers a
deliberately rare state that the baseline almost never finds (recall $\num{0.3322}$ versus
$\num{0.0151}$), with the largest margin on forward-in-time extrapolation---precisely where smoothing is
weakest. Because the comparison is inductive on both sides, this gain is the matched control whose absence
inflates symmetric-smoothing studies, not the transductive confound. A split-conformal layer supplies
distribution-free coverage ($\num{0.8968}$ against a target of $\num{0.90}$) and temperature scaling cuts
the expected calibration error by \SI{73}{\percent}. All data are synthetic; external validation on real
single-cell RNA-sequencing data is the decisive next step.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}
Single-cell measurements resolve continuous trajectories of differentiation, activation, and treatment
response, and a recurring analysis goal is to \emph{forecast} the state a cell or population will occupy
under a condition not directly observed: a donor not yet profiled, a batch run later, a later point along
a developmental trajectory, or an untested perturbation. Two structural features of these data shape any
forecaster. First, cells concentrate near a low-dimensional manifold that is routinely approximated by a
$k$-nearest-neighbor ($k$NN) graph before clustering, embedding, or pseudotime inference
\cite{wolf2018scanpy,mcinnes2018umap,becht2019umap,vandermaaten2008tsne,belkin2003laplacian,luxburg2007spectral}.
Second, the biologically decisive states---transient progenitors, treatment-resistant clones---are
frequently rare, and ordinary point classifiers, trained to minimize average error, ignore them.

The canonical response to both features is graph-based semi-supervised learning
\cite{zhu2002labelprop,zhou2004local}: predictions are allowed to diffuse over the data graph so that each
cell borrows statistical strength from its neighborhood. The diffusion operator used almost universally
is the symmetric normalized adjacency $S=D^{-1/2}AD^{-1/2}$ and its Laplacian $L=D-A$
\cite{haghverdi2016diffusion,coifman2006diffusion}. This object carries two limitations that bear directly
on transition forecasting, and that motivate the present work. (i) It is self-adjoint, hence reversible:
it cannot encode the direction of the developmental trajectory, even though pseudotime supplies exactly
that arrow \cite{trapnell2014monocle,haghverdi2016diffusion} and directed transition operators are the
basis of modern fate mapping \cite{lamanno2018velocity,lange2022cellrank}. (ii) Built over all cells, it
is transductive: it consults the held-out cells' connectivity at inference, so a measured accuracy gain
over an inductive point classifier conflates the value of diffusion with a transductive information
advantage---the confound that comparable studies flag as their decisive missing control.

We take both limitations as design targets and arrive at a new operator. Rather than re-applying label
propagation, we build the forecaster from a spectral-truncated, pseudotime-directed graph operator whose
construction contributes four innovations. (1) A \emph{directed} propagator $P$ reweights each edge
$i\!\to\!j$ by a bounded forward kernel $\kappa(t_j-t_i)$ of the pseudotime increment and row-normalizes;
$P$ is non-self-adjoint---the single-cell instance of the operator noncommutativity that recent
$C^*$-algebraic kernel machines exploit for expressiveness
\cite{hashimoto2026spectral,vansuijlekom2021spectral}---and it is the source of forward transport along
the trajectory. (2) A \emph{spectral truncation} restricts the diffusion to the leading $r$ graph-Fourier
modes, band-limiting and denoising a lazy directed walk into a closed-form solve; truncation is the graph
analog of the spectral truncation of multiplication operators in noncommutative geometry, and the
truncated product is \emph{not} the product of the truncations---the structural origin of a second,
distinct noncommutativity. (3) An out-of-sample Nystr\"om extension makes the forecaster \emph{inductive},
so the matched inductive control is the method itself rather than a missing experiment. (4) A
distribution-free \emph{conformal} layer for the rare state and a \emph{persistent-homology} topology
signal replace the passive connected-component count of prior work with a genuine multiscale descriptor.

We evaluate this operator against the symmetric smoother and a point classifier under identical,
leakage-checked conditions. All experiments run on a seeded synthetic branching lineage: this is a
controlled benchmark, not a biological dataset, and we are explicit about that throughout. Our aim is a
falsifiable, fully reproducible result that isolates what the directed, truncated, inductive operator buys
and that delineates exactly what it does and does not support. The analytical guarantees stated below are
proved in full in Appendix~\ref{sec:theory}; numerical and reproducibility details are collected in
Appendix~\ref{sec:methods}; and a self-contained, from-scratch derivation of every implemented component
is given in Appendix~\ref{sec:scratch}.

\section{The pseudotime-directed, spectrally truncated operator}
\label{sec:method}
We build the forecaster from a single non-self-adjoint operator (Fig.~\ref{fig:schematic}). This section
develops it in the order one would derive it: from points to a directed propagator, then to its
band-limited spectral truncation, then to the two distinct noncommutativities the construction creates,
and finally to the inductive extension and conformal layer that make it a usable, falsifiable forecaster.

\subsection{From points to a directed propagator}
A symmetric $k$NN graph on cell features gives the adjacency $A=A^\top$ that single-cell pipelines already
build \cite{wolf2018scanpy}; its degree is $d_i=\sum_j A_{ij}$, $D=\operatorname{diag}(d_i)$, and the
standard smoother is the self-adjoint $S=D^{-1/2}AD^{-1/2}$. The arrow that $S$ discards is supplied by
pseudotime. We therefore reweight each directed edge $i\!\to\!j$ by a bounded forward kernel of the
pseudotime increment,
\begin{equation}
W_{ij}=A_{ij}\,\kappa(t_j-t_i),\qquad \kappa(\delta)=\exp\!\big(\beta\tanh(\delta/\tau)\big),
\label{eq:kernel}
\end{equation}
and row-normalize to a transition matrix $P=D_W^{-1}W$ with $(D_W)_{ii}=\sum_j W_{ij}$. The kernel
up-weights forward edges ($t_j>t_i$) and down-weights backward ones, with $\beta\ge0$ setting the
directional strength and $\tau>0$ the pseudotime scale; the $\tanh$ keeps the reweighting bounded so that
no single edge dominates. Because forward and backward edges receive different weights, $P$ is
non-self-adjoint, $PP^\top\neq P^\top P$, and the scalar $\nu(P)=\|PP^\top-P^\top P\|_F/\|P\|_F^2$
witnesses this non-normality [Theorem~\ref{thm:noncomm}(i)]. This is the property that lets $P$ transport
labels along the trajectory rather than merely smooth them across it, and it is shared by every
directed-graph operator in single-cell biology---directed Laplacians, RNA-velocity transition matrices,
fate maps \cite{chung2005directed,lamanno2018velocity,lange2022cellrank}.

\subsection{Spectral truncation: band-limiting the operator}
A directed walk on a noisy $k$NN graph still mixes high-frequency noise into the forecast. We suppress it
by spectral truncation. The graph-Fourier modes are the eigenvectors $u_1,\dots,u_n$ of $S$; the $r$ with
the largest eigenvalues are the smoothest, lowest-frequency functions on the graph (equivalently the
lowest modes of the normalized Laplacian $\mathcal L=I-S$). Collecting them in $U_r=[u_1\cdots u_r]$ and
writing $\Pi_r=U_rU_r^\top$ for the band-limiting projector, we compress a lazy directed walk
$\widetilde P=(1-\varepsilon)I+\varepsilon P$ to its action on the band-limited subspace,
\begin{equation}
B=U_r^\top\widetilde P\,U_r\in\mathbb{R}^{r\times r},
\label{eq:trunc}
\end{equation}
and solve the band-limited diffusion in closed form: seeding one-hot labels $F_0$ on the annotated cells,
the field is $F^\star=U_r(I-\alpha B)^{-1}(1-\alpha)U_r^\top F_0$ (Theorem~\ref{thm:fixedpoint}).
Truncation does double duty. It denoises---restricting to the $r$ smoothest modes is, for the
positive-semidefinite diffusion filter the method applies, the Eckart--Young--Mirsky-optimal rank-$r$
approximation, and it maximizes the retained smoothing energy $\operatorname{Tr}(\Pi_r S)$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:trunc}). And it compresses---the solve runs in $r$ coordinates rather than $n$,
$r\ll n$.

\subsection{Two distinct noncommutativities}
It is easy to conflate two structural facts about this operator, and we separate them deliberately. The
first is directedness: $P$ is non-self-adjoint because the forward kernel makes incoming and outgoing
transition mass asymmetric at any vertex with a net pseudotime gradient [Theorem~\ref{thm:noncomm}(i)].
This is the source of forward transport, but it is a property of any directed operator and is \emph{not}
special to truncation. The second is genuinely noncommutative and is created by truncation itself: for
cell-wise functions $\phi,\psi$ the truncated multiplication operators
$M_\phi^{(r)}=U_r^\top\operatorname{diag}(\phi)U_r$ do not commute, $[M_\phi^{(r)},M_\psi^{(r)}]\neq0$,
vanishing only in the full-rank limit $\Pi_r=I$ [Theorem~\ref{thm:noncomm}(ii)]. These truncated
multiplications generate a noncommutative operator $*$-algebra whose symmetric Gram object
$\mathcal K=\sum_c M_cM_c^\top$ is a positive-definite kernel on the band-limited subspace
(Theorem~\ref{thm:kernel})---the graph instance of the spectral-truncation noncommutativity that underlies
$C^*$-algebraic kernel machines \cite{hashimoto2026spectral}. We emphasize that we instantiate this
structure on the cell graph and supply the Gram kernel; we do not develop a new vector-valued
reproducing-kernel framework, and the truncation noncommutativity is a finite-rank object, not a full
$C^*$-algebraic construction.

\subsection{Making the forecaster inductive}
A diffusion built over all cells is transductive, and the resulting accuracy gain cannot be separated from
the held-out connectivity it consumes. We remove the confound by construction. The inductive forecaster
builds $A$, $U_r$, and $P$ on the training cells only and labels a held-out cell $x_\ast$ by a
Gaussian-weighted average of the training field over its nearest \emph{training} cells,
$\widehat F(x_\ast)=\sum_{i\in\mathcal N(x_\ast)}w_iF^\star_{\mathrm{tr}}(x_i)$---an out-of-sample
Nystr\"om/Nadaraya--Watson extension \cite{williams2001nystrom,bengio2004oos} that never forms a
test--test edge and never reads held-out adjacency (Theorem~\ref{thm:inductive}). The matched inductive
control is therefore the method itself, not an experiment we leave undone, and it is this inductive variant
that we put forward throughout.

\subsection{Conformal calibration and a multiscale topology signal}
Two layers complete the forecaster. A split-conformal layer holds out a calibration slice of the annotated
cells, computes nonconformity scores $1-p_y$, and thresholds at the conformal quantile to form prediction
sets with finite-sample coverage $\ge1-\delta$ under exchangeability (Theorem~\ref{thm:conformal});
temperature scaling additionally recalibrates the scalar confidence \cite{guo2017calibration}. Separately,
we replace the constant connected-component count of prior work with the degree-$0$ persistence barcode of
the cell cloud, computed exactly by union--find on the $k$NN distance graph
\cite{edelsbrunner2010computational,carlsson2009topology}; the resulting $\beta_0(\epsilon)$ curve is a
genuine multiscale descriptor, and its per-cell witness drives an active sampler that targets rare cells.

\section{Results}
\label{sec:results}
We evaluate the operator on a fully seeded synthetic branching lineage, comparing the inductive
spectral-truncated directed-diffusion (STDD) forecaster---the method---against its transductive variant,
symmetric label propagation, and a matched inductive $k$NN baseline, under four leakage-checked transfer
protocols. Every reported number is generated verbatim from the run artifact
\texttt{results/summary.json} (Appendix~\ref{sec:methods}); none is typed by hand.

\subsection{A controlled lineage with replicate, condition, and pseudotime structure}
Our benchmark reproduces the replicate, condition, and pseudotime structure a real forecaster must handle
while remaining bit-for-bit reproducible (Figs.~\ref{fig:schematic} and \ref{fig:lineage}). Cells live in
a low-dimensional feature space---a stand-in for a PCA or latent embedding of expression---and progress
along a tree of three branches (a common progenitor and two fates that split at pseudotime $0.45$) as
Gaussian blobs whose mean drifts along each branch; early cells overlap and are hard to separate, while
late cells are well resolved. Each cell carries the metadata a forecaster would use---branch/state,
pseudotime, donor, batch, and perturbation condition---assigned independently of the biological branch so
that the held-out splits are nontrivial. One state is carved from the late tip of the second fate to be
deliberately rare, comprising \SI{4}{\percent} of cells. The full-scale configuration generates
\num{3000} cells with \num{16} features, \num{8} donors, \num{4} batches, and \num{5} perturbation
conditions over \num{5} random seeds; the run completed in \SI{32.0}{\second} with a peak memory of
\SI{621.2}{\mega\byte}. Across seeds the integrity gates passed: the grouping splits were leakage-free,
the rare state was present, and the directed propagator was non-normal.

\subsection{The operator is genuinely noncommutative, and truncation is well posed}
We first confirm that the constructed operator has the structural properties the method relies on
(Fig.~\ref{fig:operator}). The directed propagator is non-normal: its relative non-normality is
$\nu(P)=\num{0.0370}$, and directional reweighting strictly increases it over the undirected random walk
[$\nu(P_0)=\num{0.0272}$, itself nonzero because degree-asymmetric row normalization already breaks
normality], so the arrow of pseudotime contributes a measurable \SI{36}{\percent} of additional
non-normality [Fig.~\ref{fig:operator}(a)]. This directedness is distinct from the truncation
noncommutativity, which is a separate and genuinely noncommutative object: the truncated multiplication
operators satisfy $\|[M_\phi^{(r)},M_\psi^{(r)}]\|_F=\num{5.93}$ [Theorem~\ref{thm:noncomm}(ii)], and the
Gram kernel $\mathcal K=\sum_c M_cM_c^\top$ they generate has smallest eigenvalue
$\lambda_{\min}=\num{1.62}>0$ (Theorem~\ref{thm:kernel}), so it is strictly positive definite. The
graph-Fourier spectrum of $S$ decays smoothly from $1$ to $\approx\num{0.41}$ over the first \num{80}
modes [Fig.~\ref{fig:operator}(b)], so a rank-$r$ truncation retains the low-frequency, label-relevant
structure while discarding high-frequency noise; sweeping the rank reveals an interior operating
point---accuracy is flat near $\approx\num{0.71}$ while rare-state recall peaks around $r\in[64,80]$
[Fig.~\ref{fig:operator}(c)], the bias--variance trade-off that Theorem~\ref{thm:trunc} formalizes. We fix
$r=\num{80}$ throughout, at which the diffusion is well posed (spectral radius
$\rho(\alpha B)=\num{0.9209}<1$, Theorem~\ref{thm:fixedpoint}).

\subsection{The inductive operator beats a matched point classifier on every protocol}
Under all four leakage-checked protocols, the inductive STDD forecaster predicts held-out cell states more
accurately than the inductive $k$NN baseline that receives the same annotated cells and the same
neighborhood radius [Table~\ref{tab:main}, Fig.~\ref{fig:forecast}(a)]. Accuracy rises on the donor split
from \num{0.6550} to \num{0.7227} ($+\num{0.0677}$), on the batch split from \num{0.6355} to \num{0.7134}
($+\num{0.0779}$), on the perturbation split from \num{0.6490} to \num{0.6741} ($+\num{0.0251}$), and on
the time split---forward-in-time extrapolation, the hardest protocol---from \num{0.4574} to \num{0.6323}
($+\num{0.1749}$). Averaged over the four splits the inductive operator reaches \num{0.6856} against
\num{0.5992} for the baseline, a mean gain of \num{0.0864}, and it improves on \emph{every} split rather
than on average only. The comparison is inductive on both sides: the STDD field is built on the training
cells and extended to held-out cells by the Nystr\"om step (Theorem~\ref{thm:inductive}), so the gain is
not the transductive-versus-inductive asymmetry that inflates symmetric-smoothing studies. Strikingly, the
same inductive operator even exceeds the transductive symmetric label-propagation baseline on three of
four splits and in the mean (\num{0.6856} versus \num{0.6587}), despite operating in the strictly harder
inductive regime.

\subsection{Directional transport recovers a rare state and rescues forward extrapolation}
The clearer effect is on the rare state and on the trajectory's forward direction
[Fig.~\ref{fig:forecast}(b)]. The point classifier essentially never recovers the rare population (mean
rare-state recall \num{0.0151}); the inductive operator raises it to \num{0.3322}, a \num{0.3171} absolute
gain, recovering the rare state on the donor (\num{0.3968}), batch (\num{0.5706}), and perturbation
(\num{0.3616}) splits. The directional mechanism is most visible on the time split, where train and test
are separated along pseudotime: there the directed operator transports early labels forward to raise
accuracy by \num{0.1749} over the point classifier and by \num{0.0850} over symmetric label propagation,
the largest accuracy margin of any split. Rare-state recall on the time split remains \num{0.0000} for
every method: the rare late-tip cells lie entirely beyond the labeled training distribution, so no
diffusion can manufacture a class that was never reachable from the annotated set
(Corollary~\ref{cor:nonident})---we report this null as is. The directional contribution to rare recovery
is isolated by the transductive operator, which holds the transductive/inductive setting fixed against
symmetric propagation and nearly doubles its rare-state recall (\num{0.4418} versus \num{0.2383} mean;
donor \num{0.5925} versus \num{0.1520}).

\subsection{Conformal calibration gives distribution-free coverage and removes the ECE cost}
The row-normalized band-limited field yields under-dispersed (over-smoothed) class probabilities whose
confidence understates the model's accuracy: the raw inductive forecaster has an expected calibration
error of \num{0.1374}, and the temperature that minimizes calibration negative log-likelihood is
$T=\num{0.55}<1$---the probabilities must be \emph{sharpened}, not softened. A split-conformal layer and
this temperature scaling, fit on a held-out calibration slice of the annotated cells, repair the
miscalibration with a distribution-free guarantee (Fig.~\ref{fig:calibration},
Theorem~\ref{thm:conformal}). On an exchangeable hold-out the conformal prediction sets attain a realized
marginal coverage of \num{0.8968} against the target $1-\delta=\num{0.90}$, with rare-state coverage
\num{0.9307} and an average set size of \num{1.81} classes out of four---so the guarantee is met without
collapsing to uninformative sets. Temperature scaling cuts the expected calibration error from \num{0.1374}
to \num{0.0369}, a \SI{73}{\percent} reduction, turning the calibration penalty of graph smoothing into a
calibration guarantee. We stress that the coverage guarantee holds under exchangeability of calibration and
test cells; under the grouping-transfer splits, where the held-out group induces a distribution shift,
coverage is only approximate, and we report the exchangeable-hold-out figure as the regime in which the
theorem applies.

\subsection{Persistence-guided active sampling recovers the rare state}
Replacing the passive connected-component count of prior work with the degree-$0$ persistence barcode turns
topology into an informative, scale-aware signal. The $\beta_0(\epsilon)$ curve of the cell cloud falls
from \num{3000} components at scale $\epsilon=1.0$ to $9$ at $\epsilon=3.5$ (Fig.~\ref{fig:lineage}, inset),
and the longest bars flag the well-separated pockets where rare cells reside. A persistence-guided
active-sampling policy---scoring unlabeled cells by forecast uncertainty times per-cell topological
isolation---raises rare-state recall on the remaining unlabeled cells to \num{0.3941} ($\pm\num{0.1910}$),
against \num{0.0481} ($\pm\num{0.0445}$) for uniform random querying and \num{0.3556} for the
inverse-density policy of prior work, spending \SI{40.0}{\percent} of its queries on genuinely rare cells
against a \SI{4}{\percent} base rate [Table~\ref{tab:extended}, Fig.~\ref{fig:forecast}(c)].

\section{Discussion and conclusion}
\label{sec:discussion}
Within a controlled synthetic benchmark, conditioning single-cell forecasts on a pseudotime-directed,
spectrally truncated graph operator helps---and helps in a way the symmetric smoother cannot. The
directional, non-self-adjoint propagator transports labels along the trajectory, which both recovers a rare
cell state that a matched point classifier almost entirely misses and, uniquely, rescues forward-in-time
extrapolation. The spectral truncation band-limits the operator into a denoised, closed-form solve whose
leading modes are provably the optimal low-pass approximation. Most critically, the out-of-sample Nystr\"om
extension makes the comparison inductive: the method beats the point classifier on every transfer protocol
without ever seeing held-out connectivity, so the accuracy gain is not the transductive information
advantage that inflates symmetric-smoothing studies---it is the matched inductive control those studies
identify as their decisive missing experiment. The conformal layer converts the calibration penalty of
graph smoothing into a distribution-free coverage guarantee, and the degree-$0$ persistence barcode
replaces a passive connected-component count with an informative, scale-aware topology signal that guides
active recovery of the rare state.

These results are consistent with the long-standing intuition behind graph-based semi-supervised learning
\cite{zhou2004local,zhu2002labelprop} and with the central role of directed transition operators in
single-cell fate mapping \cite{lamanno2018velocity,lange2022cellrank}; the methodological advance is to
build the forecaster from a spectrally truncated, pseudotime-directed operator, in the spirit of
spectral-truncation kernel machines \cite{hashimoto2026spectral,vansuijlekom2021spectral}, and to make it
inductive and conformally calibrated. We are precise about how this relates to that line of work: we
instantiate spectral truncation and operator noncommutativity on the cell graph and supply a
positive-definite noncommutative Gram kernel (Theorem~\ref{thm:kernel}), but we do \emph{not} develop a new
vector-valued reproducing-kernel learning framework or a deep-learning extension as that work does. Our
contribution lies along an orthogonal axis---a directed (non-self-adjoint) transport operator made
inductive and conformally calibrated for single-cell transition forecasting---and the kernel $\mathcal K$
enters as the structural Gram object rather than as a learned reproducing-kernel Hilbert space (RKHS). The
gains are real but should not be oversold, and the limitations below bound every claim above.

\subsection{Limitations}
\label{sec:limitations}
We list the limitations plainly because they bound every claim above.
(1) \emph{Synthetic data.} All numbers come from a generated branching lineage, not biological
measurements; the magnitudes need not transfer to real scRNA-seq, where noise, dropout, and confounding are
far more severe.
(2) \emph{The transductive operator trades some accuracy.} Held fixed in the transductive setting, the
directed operator nearly doubles symmetric label propagation's rare-state recall but loses a few points of
mean accuracy; it is the inductive variant that recovers and exceeds the baseline's accuracy while removing
the confound, and it is that variant we put forward as the method.
(3) \emph{Calibration is repaired post hoc, under exchangeability.} The conformal guarantee holds for
exchangeable calibration and test cells; under grouping-transfer distribution shift, realized coverage is
only approximate.
(4) \emph{Directionality requires a pseudotime estimate.} The operator consumes a pseudotime coordinate; on
real data this is itself inferred and noisy, and a mis-estimated arrow would weaken or mislead the
directional transport.
(5) \emph{Standard degree-$0$ topology.} We use the exact $H_0$ persistence barcode---standard persistent
homology \cite{edelsbrunner2010computational,carlsson2009topology} applied here as a multiscale,
scale-aware replacement for the connected-component count, not a new topological construction---and not
higher-order ($H_1$, $H_2$) persistence; the truncation-commutator noncommutativity and the Gram kernel
$\mathcal K$ are finite-rank, not a full $C^*$-algebraic construction.
(6) \emph{The directional prior is aligned with the generator.} The forward kernel encodes the same arrow
of pseudotime that the synthetic lineage is built around, so part of the forward-in-time accuracy gain
reflects a correctly specified prior; on real data with inferred, noisy pseudotime the advantage may be
smaller. The fixed-kernel transport is also not learned: a learned directed propagator and an RKHS use of
$\mathcal K$ are left to future work.
(7) \emph{Single benchmark family.} One synthetic generator, however controlled, cannot substitute for the
diversity of real datasets; external validation remains the decisive test. The natural next step is to
repeat exactly this comparison on real scRNA-seq and perturbation-screen data, for which the repository
provides honest, unexercised ingest stubs.

\section{Theory of spectral-truncated noncommutative graph operators}
\label{sec:theory}
We now make precise, and prove in full, the structural properties the forecaster relies upon: that the
symmetric backbone is positive semidefinite and that its leading modes are the optimal band-limit
(Lemma~\ref{lem:dirichlet}, Theorem~\ref{thm:trunc}); that the directed propagator is genuinely non-normal
and that spectral truncation breaks the commutativity of multiplication (Theorems~\ref{thm:noncomm} and
\ref{thm:kernel}); that the truncated directed diffusion is well posed with a closed form that the code
solves directly (Theorem~\ref{thm:fixedpoint}); that the Nystr\"om extension is inductive and consistent
(Theorem~\ref{thm:inductive}); and that the conformal layer has distribution-free coverage
(Theorem~\ref{thm:conformal}). Throughout, $\mathbf{1}$ is the all-ones vector,
$\|\cdot\|_2,\|\cdot\|_F$ the Euclidean and Frobenius norms, $\operatorname{Tr}$ the trace, and
$X\succeq0$ ($X\succ0$) positive (semi)definiteness.

\begin{definition}[Cell graph, directed propagator, graph-Fourier basis, truncation]
\label{def:graph}
Let $A=A^\top\in\mathbb{R}^{n\times n}$ be the symmetric adjacency of the $k$NN cell graph ($A_{ij}\ge0$,
$A_{ii}=0$), $d_i=\sum_j A_{ij}$ the degree, $D=\operatorname{diag}(d_i)$, and $L=D-A$ the combinatorial
Laplacian. Write $S=D^{-1/2}AD^{-1/2}$ for the symmetric normalized adjacency. For a pseudotime vector
$t\in\mathbb{R}^n$, a directional strength $\beta\ge0$, and a scale $\tau>0$, the directed propagator is
$P=D_W^{-1}W$ where $W_{ij}=A_{ij}\,\kappa(t_j-t_i)$, $\kappa(\delta)=\exp(\beta\tanh(\delta/\tau))$, and
$(D_W)_{ii}=\sum_j W_{ij}$; $P$ is row-stochastic. Let $U_r=[u_1\cdots u_r]\in\mathbb{R}^{n\times r}$
collect the eigenvectors of $S$ for its $r$ largest eigenvalues (equivalently the $r$ smoothest,
lowest-frequency modes of the normalized Laplacian $\mathcal L=I-S$), $\Pi_r=U_rU_r^\top$ the
band-limiting projector, $\widetilde P=(1-\varepsilon)I+\varepsilon P$ the lazy directed walk for laziness
$\varepsilon\in(0,1]$, and $B=U_r^\top\widetilde P\,U_r=U_r^\top\!\big((1-\varepsilon)I+\varepsilon
P\big)U_r$ the truncated lazy directed transport---the $r\times r$ coordinate representation of the
compression $\Pi_r\widetilde P\,\Pi_r$ on the band-limited subspace.
\end{definition}

\begin{lemma}[Dirichlet energy and positive semidefiniteness]
\label{lem:dirichlet}
For every $f\in\mathbb{R}^{n}$, $f^\top L f=\tfrac12\sum_{i,j} A_{ij}(f_i-f_j)^2\ge0$, so $L\succeq0$; and
$S=I-D^{-1/2}LD^{-1/2}$ has every eigenvalue in $[-1,1]$.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Expanding $f^\top Lf=\sum_i d_i f_i^2-\sum_{i,j}A_{ij}f_if_j$ and symmetrizing the diagonal term with
$A_{ij}=A_{ji}$ gives $f^\top Lf=\tfrac12\sum_{i,j}A_{ij}(f_i-f_j)^2\ge0$ since $A_{ij}\ge0$, so
$L\succeq0$. The normalized Laplacian $\mathcal L=D^{-1/2}LD^{-1/2}$ is congruent to $L$ through the
invertible $D^{-1/2}$, hence $\mathcal L\succeq0$ and $S=I-\mathcal L\preceq I$, giving $\lambda(S)\le1$.
The signless identity $f^\top(D+A)f=\tfrac12\sum_{i,j}A_{ij}(f_i+f_j)^2\ge0$ gives $D+A\succeq0$, so
$I+S=D^{-1/2}(D+A)D^{-1/2}\succeq0$ and $\lambda(S)\ge-1$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Spectral truncation is the optimal band-limit]
\label{thm:trunc}
Among all rank-$r$ orthogonal projectors $\Pi$, the band-limiting projector $\Pi_r=U_rU_r^\top$ of
Definition~\ref{def:graph} maximizes the retained smoothing energy $\operatorname{Tr}(\Pi S)$. Moreover,
for any positive-semidefinite graph low-pass filter $R=g(S)$ with $g$ nondecreasing on $[-1,1]$---in
particular the diffusion resolvent $R=(I-\alpha S)^{-1}\succ0$ and the heat kernel $R=e^{-t(I-S)}\succeq0$
that the method's smoothing realizes---$\Pi_r$ furnishes the Eckart--Young--Mirsky-optimal rank-$r$
approximation of $R$: it minimizes $\|R-\Pi R\Pi\|$ over all rank-$r$ orthogonal projectors in every
unitarily invariant norm, with spectral-norm error $g(\lambda_{r+1})$. (For the indefinite $S$ itself,
low-pass truncation is optimal among band-limited projectors but is not in general the unconstrained best
rank-$r$ approximation, which would retain the largest-magnitude rather than the largest-value
eigenvalues; the low-pass choice is the correct one because the forecaster applies the monotone PSD filter
$R$, not $S$.)
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Write $S=\sum_j\lambda_j u_ju_j^\top$ with $\lambda_1\ge\cdots\ge\lambda_n$. For a rank-$r$ projector
$\Pi$, $\operatorname{Tr}(\Pi S)=\sum_j\lambda_j\,u_j^\top\Pi u_j$ with $0\le u_j^\top\Pi u_j\le1$ and
$\sum_j u_j^\top\Pi u_j=\operatorname{Tr}\Pi=r$; this linear objective over the polytope
$\{0\le c_j\le1,\sum_j c_j=r\}$ is maximized by putting unit weight on the $r$ largest $\lambda_j$, i.e.,
$\Pi=\Pi_r$ (Ky Fan). For $R=g(S)=\sum_j g(\lambda_j)u_ju_j^\top$ with $g$ nondecreasing, the eigenvalues
$g(\lambda_j)\ge0$ are ordered with the $\lambda_j$, so the $r$ largest belong to $u_1,\dots,u_r$;
Eckart--Young--Mirsky then gives $\Pi_rR\Pi_r=\sum_{j\le r}g(\lambda_j)u_ju_j^\top$ as the best rank-$r$
approximation of the positive-semidefinite $R$ in every unitarily invariant norm, with
$\|R-\Pi_rR\Pi_r\|_2=g(\lambda_{r+1})$. The resolvent $g(\lambda)=1/(1-\alpha\lambda)$ and heat kernel
$g(\lambda)=e^{-t(1-\lambda)}$ are nondecreasing and positive on $[-1,1]$ for $\alpha\in(0,1),\,t>0$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:fixedpoint} gives $I-\alpha S\succ0$), so the truncated diffusion the method runs is
optimally band-limited.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Noncommutativity of the directed propagator and of spectral truncation]
\label{thm:noncomm}
(i) The directed propagator $P$ is non-normal whenever its outgoing and incoming squared transition mass
differ at some vertex: the commutator diagonal is $[P,P^\top]_{ii}=\sum_j P_{ij}^2-\sum_j P_{ji}^2$, so
$[P,P^\top]\neq0$, and hence $\nu(P)=\|[P,P^\top]\|_F/\|P\|_F^2>0$, as soon as $\sum_j P_{ij}^2\neq\sum_j
P_{ji}^2$ for some $i$; the forward kernel makes the incoming and outgoing reweighting at a vertex with a
net pseudotime gradient asymmetric, so this holds and $\nu(P)$ strictly exceeds the undirected control
$\nu(P_0)$.
(ii) Spectral truncation breaks the commutativity of pointwise multiplication: for cell-wise functions
$\phi,\psi$ the truncated multiplication operators $M_\phi^{(r)}=U_r^\top\!\operatorname{diag}(\phi)U_r$
satisfy
\begin{widetext}
\begin{equation}
[M_\phi^{(r)},M_\psi^{(r)}]=U_r^\top\operatorname{diag}(\phi)(\Pi_r-I)\operatorname{diag}(\psi)U_r
-U_r^\top\operatorname{diag}(\psi)(\Pi_r-I)\operatorname{diag}(\phi)U_r\neq0
\label{eq:commutator}
\end{equation}
\end{widetext}
in general, with the operator-norm bound $\|[M_\phi^{(r)},M_\psi^{(r)}]\|_2\le 2\|\phi\|_\infty
\|\psi\|_\infty$ (the Frobenius norm can grow as $\sqrt{r}$ across the band), vanishing only in the
full-rank limit $\Pi_r=I$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
(i) The diagonal entries of the two products are $(PP^\top)_{ii}=\sum_j P_{ij}^2$ (the squared outgoing
transition mass at $i$) and $(P^\top P)_{ii}=\sum_j P_{ji}^2$ (the squared incoming mass), so
$[P,P^\top]_{ii}=\sum_j(P_{ij}^2-P_{ji}^2)$. If $P$ were normal the commutator would vanish, forcing
$\sum_j P_{ij}^2=\sum_j P_{ji}^2$ at every vertex. Row-stochasticity fixes the row sums $\sum_j P_{ij}=1$
but constrains neither the column sums nor the squared masses; the directional reweighting
$W_{ij}=A_{ij}\exp(\beta\tanh((t_j-t_i)/\tau))$ up-weights forward edges and down-weights backward ones,
so a vertex with a net forward (or backward) pseudotime gradient has unequal incoming and outgoing squared
mass and a nonzero commutator diagonal. Hence $[P,P^\top]\neq0$ and $\nu(P)>0$; the undirected control
$\beta=0$ retains only the residual degree-normalization asymmetry of $D_W^{-1}W$, giving the smaller but
still positive $\nu(P_0)$ reported in Table~\ref{tab:extended}.
(ii) Writing $\operatorname{diag}(\phi)\operatorname{diag}(\psi)=\operatorname{diag}(\phi\psi)
=\operatorname{diag}(\psi)\operatorname{diag}(\phi)$ and inserting $I=\Pi_r+(I-\Pi_r)$ between the two
diagonal factors,
\begin{widetext}
\begin{equation}
M_\phi^{(r)}M_\psi^{(r)}=U_r^\top\operatorname{diag}(\phi)\Pi_r\operatorname{diag}(\psi)U_r
=U_r^\top\operatorname{diag}(\phi\psi)U_r-U_r^\top\operatorname{diag}(\phi)(I-\Pi_r)\operatorname{diag}(\psi)U_r,
\end{equation}
\end{widetext}
and symmetrically for $M_\psi^{(r)}M_\phi^{(r)}$. Subtracting gives the commutator~\eqref{eq:commutator},
whose two terms cancel only when $(I-\Pi_r)\operatorname{diag}(\psi)U_r$ and
$(I-\Pi_r)\operatorname{diag}(\phi)U_r$ are aligned---generically false for $r<n$. The bound follows from
$\|U_r\|_2=1$, $\|\operatorname{diag}(\phi)\|_2=\|\phi\|_\infty$, and $\|I-\Pi_r\|_2=1$; at $r=n$,
$\Pi_r=I$ and the commutator is $U^\top[\operatorname{diag}(\phi),\operatorname{diag}(\psi)]U=0$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[A positive-definite noncommutative truncated operator kernel]
\label{thm:kernel}
For the feature channels $\phi_1,\dots,\phi_d$ (the columns of $X$) with truncated multiplication operators
$M_c=M_{\phi_c}^{(r)}=U_r^\top\operatorname{diag}(\phi_c)U_r$, the truncated operator kernel
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:nckernel}
\mathcal K=\sum_{c=1}^{d} M_c M_c^\top
\end{equation}
is positive semidefinite, $\mathcal K\succeq0$, and positive definite whenever the truncated
multiplications share no common left-null vector. Its generators $M_c$ do not pairwise commute
[Theorem~\ref{thm:noncomm}(ii)], so $\mathcal K$ is the symmetric Gram object of a genuinely
noncommutative truncated operator $*$-algebra on the cell graph---the graph instance of the noncommutative
operator structure underlying spectral-truncation kernel machines \cite{hashimoto2026spectral}. At the
reported scale $\lambda_{\min}(\mathcal K)=\num{1.62}>0$ (Table~\ref{tab:extended}), so $\mathcal K\succ0$
is strictly positive definite.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
For any $v$, $v^\top\mathcal K v=\sum_c v^\top M_cM_c^\top v=\sum_c\|M_c^\top v\|_2^2\ge0$, so
$\mathcal K\succeq0$ as a sum of Gram terms. Equality forces $M_c^\top v=0$ for every $c$, i.e., $v$ is a
common left-null vector of the truncated multiplications; excluding this (which the strictly positive
$\lambda_{\min}(\mathcal K)$ confirms) gives $\mathcal K\succ0$. The noncommutativity of the generators is
Theorem~\ref{thm:noncomm}(ii). Because $\mathcal K$ is symmetric positive (semi)definite it defines a valid
kernel on the band-limited subspace whose off-diagonal structure is governed by the non-vanishing
commutators $[M_b,M_c]$, which is what distinguishes it from a separable (commutative) construction.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Well-posedness and closed form of the truncated directed diffusion]
\label{thm:fixedpoint}
Fix $\alpha\in(0,1)$ and write $\rho=\rho(\alpha B)$ for the spectral radius of the truncated transport
$\alpha B$. If $\rho<1$ then $I-\alpha B$ is invertible, the reduced normal equations
$(I-\alpha B)\,c=(1-\alpha)U_r^\top F_0$ have the unique solution
$c^\star=(1-\alpha)(I-\alpha B)^{-1}U_r^\top F_0$, the lifted field $F^\star=U_rc^\star$ is the limit of
the band-limited power iteration $c^{(m+1)}=\alpha Bc^{(m)}+(1-\alpha)U_r^\top F_0$ from any start, and the
error contracts as $\rho^{m}$ up to a constant. A simple sufficient condition is
$\alpha\,\sigma_{\max}(B)<1$ with $\sigma_{\max}(B)\le(1-\varepsilon)+\varepsilon\,\sigma_{\max}(P)$; this
bound is conservative because $P$ is row-stochastic with $\sigma_{\max}(P)$ possibly exceeding $1$, and the
operative quantity is the spectral radius, which at the reported configuration is $\rho=\num{0.9209}<1$
(Table~\ref{tab:extended}, integrity gate \texttt{diffusion\_well\_posed}). Moreover $F^\star$ is invariant
to the sign gauge of $U_r$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
If $\rho(\alpha B)<1$ then $\alpha B$ has no eigenvalue equal to $1$, so $I-\alpha B$ is nonsingular and the
Neumann series $\sum_{m\ge0}(\alpha B)^m=(I-\alpha B)^{-1}$ converges (its terms decay geometrically once
$\|(\alpha B)^m\|^{1/m}\to\rho<1$ by Gelfand's formula). Subtracting the fixed-point relation
$c^\star=\alpha Bc^\star+(1-\alpha)U_r^\top F_0$ from the iteration gives
$c^{(m+1)}-c^\star=\alpha B(c^{(m)}-c^\star)$, so $c^{(m)}-c^\star=(\alpha B)^m(c^{(0)}-c^\star)\to0$ at the
asymptotic rate $\rho$. For the sufficient condition, $\rho(\alpha B)\le\alpha\,\sigma_{\max}(B)$ (spectral
radius bounded by spectral norm), and $\sigma_{\max}(B)\le(1-\varepsilon)+\varepsilon\,
\sigma_{\max}(U_r^\top PU_r)\le(1-\varepsilon)+\varepsilon\,\sigma_{\max}(P)$ by the triangle inequality and
the fact that a compression by the orthonormal $U_r$ cannot increase the largest singular value. The
closed-form solve the code runs requires only $\rho<1$, which the integrity gate verifies. Finally,
replacing $U_r$ by $U_rG$ for a diagonal sign matrix $G=G^{-1}$ sends $B\mapsto GBG$ and
$U_r^\top F_0\mapsto GU_r^\top F_0$, whence $c^\star\mapsto Gc^\star$ and
$F^\star=U_rc^\star\mapsto U_rGGc^\star=U_rc^\star$ is unchanged.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Non-identifiability of a label-free region]
\label{cor:nonident}
If a class is absent from the labeled seed $F_0$ on every cell reachable (in the band-limited field) from
the test region, the diffusion assigns it zero mass there and cannot recover it. In particular, when the
held-out test set lies beyond the labeled support along pseudotime---as on the time split---rare cells
confined to that region are unrecoverable by smoothing alone, which is the mechanism behind the null
time-split rare-state recall.
\end{corollary}
\begin{proof}
$F^\star=(1-\alpha)U_r(I-\alpha B)^{-1}U_r^\top F_0$ is linear in $F_0$; if the $c$-th column of $F_0$ is
zero on the labeled set then that column of $U_r^\top F_0$ is the projection of a vector supported off the
reachable region, and the band-limited propagation, whose support is contained in the closure of the
labeled region under the directed transport, leaves the test rows of that column at zero. The argmax
read-out can then never select the absent class on those rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Inductive consistency of the Nystr\"om extension]
\label{thm:inductive}
Let $F^\star_{\mathrm{tr}}$ be the band-limited field on the training cells and, for a held-out cell
$x_\ast$, let $\widehat F(x_\ast)=\sum_{i\in\mathcal N(x_\ast)} w_i\,F^\star_{\mathrm{tr}}(x_i)$ be its
Gaussian-weighted average over the $k$ nearest \emph{training} cells, $\sum_i w_i=1$. The estimator uses no
test--test edge and no held-out connectivity, so the forecaster is inductive. If the field is
$L$-Lipschitz on the data manifold, then $\|\widehat F(x_\ast)-F^\star(x_\ast)\|_2\le
L\max_{i\in\mathcal N(x_\ast)}\|x_i-x_\ast\|_2$, which tends to $0$ as the training sample becomes dense;
hence $\widehat F\to F^\star$ pointwise under standard manifold-density conditions.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The estimator reads only the training field and the test-to-train affinities; it never forms an edge
between two test cells nor consults the held-out adjacency, so by construction inference on $x_\ast$ is
independent of all other test cells---the definition of an inductive predictor. For the bound, convexity of
the weighted average and the triangle inequality give
\begin{widetext}
\begin{equation}
\|\widehat F(x_\ast)-F^\star(x_\ast)\|_2=\Big\|\textstyle\sum_i w_i\big(F^\star(x_i)-F^\star(x_\ast)\big)\Big\|_2
\le\sum_i w_i\|F^\star(x_i)-F^\star(x_\ast)\|_2\le L\sum_i w_i\|x_i-x_\ast\|_2\le L\max_i\|x_i-x_\ast\|_2.
\end{equation}
\end{widetext}
As the sample density increases the $k$NN radius $\max_i\|x_i-x_\ast\|_2\to0$ almost surely under standard
conditions, so the right-hand side vanishes.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Distribution-free conformal coverage]
\label{thm:conformal}
Let the nonconformity score of a cell with predicted probabilities $p$ and true class $y$ be $s=1-p_y$,
computed by the fitted forecaster. Given $n_{\mathrm{cal}}$ calibration cells exchangeable with a test
cell, let $\hat q$ be the $\lceil(n_{\mathrm{cal}}+1)(1-\delta)\rceil/n_{\mathrm{cal}}$ empirical quantile
of the calibration scores and form the prediction set $C(x)=\{c:1-p_c(x)\le\hat q\}$. Then
$\Pr[y_{\mathrm{test}}\in C(x_{\mathrm{test}})]\ge1-\delta$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
By exchangeability of the $n_{\mathrm{cal}}+1$ scores
$\{s_1,\dots,s_{n_{\mathrm{cal}}},s_{\mathrm{test}}\}$, the rank of $s_{\mathrm{test}}$ among them is
uniform on $\{1,\dots,n_{\mathrm{cal}}+1\}$, so $\Pr[s_{\mathrm{test}}\le\hat q]\ge
\lceil(n_{\mathrm{cal}}+1)(1-\delta)\rceil/(n_{\mathrm{cal}}+1)\ge1-\delta$, where $\hat q$ is the
$\lceil(n_{\mathrm{cal}}+1)(1-\delta)\rceil$-th smallest calibration score. Since
$y_{\mathrm{test}}\in C(x_{\mathrm{test}})\iff s_{\mathrm{test}}=1-p_{y_{\mathrm{test}}}\le\hat q$, the
event has probability at least $1-\delta$.
\end{proof}

\section{Numerical methods and reproducibility}
\label{sec:methods}

\paragraph{Synthetic lineage.}
Cells are generated in an $n_\text{features}$-dimensional space along a tree of three branches (a trunk
progenitor and two fates that split at pseudotime $t_0=0.45$). Branch means drift from fixed,
well-separated anchors with progression in pseudotime; each cell is the branch mean plus isotropic Gaussian
noise of standard deviation \texttt{noise}. The discrete state equals the branch for the common branches;
the rare state is a new label assigned to a random subset of the late tip ($t>0.8$) of the second fate,
whose feature vectors are additionally nudged into a distinct pocket. Donor, batch, and perturbation labels
are drawn independently of branch so that grouping splits are nontrivial. The full-scale configuration is
$n_\text{cells}=\num{3000}$, $n_\text{features}=\num{16}$, $n_\text{branches}=3$, $n_\text{donors}=8$,
$n_\text{batches}=4$, $n_\text{perturbations}=5$, $\texttt{noise}=0.7$, $\texttt{rare\_fraction}=0.04$,
$k=\num{20}$, $\texttt{label\_fraction}=0.03$, over $n_\text{seeds}=5$ seeds. Generation is fully seeded and
bit-for-bit reproducible.

\paragraph{Directed propagator and spectral truncation.}
A symmetric $k$NN graph is built on cell features (either-direction symmetrization), exactly as standard
single-cell pipelines do \cite{wolf2018scanpy}. The directed propagator reweights each edge $i\!\to\!j$ by
$\kappa(t_j-t_i)=\exp(\beta\tanh((t_j-t_i)/\tau))$ and row-normalizes, with $\beta=1.0$ and $\tau=0.2$. The
graph-Fourier basis $U_r$ is the leading $r=\num{80}$ eigenvectors of $S=D^{-1/2}AD^{-1/2}$, computed with
a fixed starting vector for determinism. The truncated lazy transport is
$B=U_r^\top((1-\varepsilon)I+\varepsilon P)U_r$ with $\varepsilon=0.6$, and the band-limited field is
$F^\star=U_r(I-\alpha B)^{-1}(1-\alpha)U_r^\top F_0$ with $\alpha=0.9$ and $F_0$ the one-hot seed on
annotated cells (Theorem~\ref{thm:fixedpoint}).

\paragraph{Forecasters.}
The transductive STDD forecaster builds the graph and basis over all cells; the inductive STDD forecaster
(the method) builds them on the training cells only and labels held-out cells by a Gaussian-weighted
average of the training field over each test cell's nearest \emph{training} cells, so held-out connectivity
is never used (Theorem~\ref{thm:inductive}). The symmetric label-propagation baseline is the transductive
normalized diffusion of Zhou \textit{et al.}~\cite{zhou2004local} ($\alpha=0.9$, $30$ iterations). The
point baseline is a plain inductive $k$NN classifier with the same $k$. All read-outs row-normalize the
non-negative part of the field to a probability simplex; the maximum class probability is the confidence
used for calibration.

\paragraph{Splits and annotation.}
Four leakage-checked protocols evaluate transfer: donor and batch (hold out whole groups), time (train on
early pseudotime, test on late---a forward-in-time extrapolation), and perturbation (hold out a whole
condition). For grouping splits the test suite asserts that no held-out group appears in both train and
test. Only \texttt{label\_fraction} of the train pool is annotated, lightly stratified so that a few labels
exist for each present state, including the rare one.

\paragraph{Conformal calibration.}
On an exchangeable random hold-out, the inductive forecaster is fit on the annotated train cells and
calibrated on an annotated calibration slice: a single temperature is fit by grid-minimizing calibration
negative log-likelihood, and the split-conformal threshold is the
$\lceil(n_{\mathrm{cal}}+1)(1-\delta)\rceil/n_{\mathrm{cal}}$ quantile of the calibration nonconformity
scores $1-p_y$, with $\delta=0.1$ (Theorem~\ref{thm:conformal}). Realized coverage, set size, and
raw-versus-calibrated ECE are measured on the test cells.

\paragraph{Persistence and active sampling.}
The degree-$0$ persistence barcode of the cell cloud is computed exactly by union--find on the $k$NN
distance graph (the finite $H_0$ bars are the minimum-spanning-tree edge weights), giving the
$\beta_0(\epsilon)$ curve and barcode statistics. The persistence-guided active-sampling policy scores
unlabeled cells by forecast uncertainty (entropy) times per-cell topological isolation (the $k$-th
nearest-neighbor distance, the per-cell witness of a long $H_0$ bar) and greedily takes the top
\texttt{active\_budget}$=\num{60}$ cells; baselines are the inverse-density policy of prior work and
uniform random querying. Rare-state recall on the remaining unlabeled cells is the lift.

\paragraph{Metrics, aggregation, and reproducibility.}
Accuracy is the fraction of held-out cells classified correctly; ECE is the population-weighted gap between
mean confidence and accuracy over ten bins; rare-state recall is the fraction of true rare cells recovered
(returning $1$ when a test set contains no rare cells). The non-normality witness is
$\nu(P)=\|PP^\top-P^\top P\|_F/\|P\|_F^2$. All metrics are averaged over $5$ seeds with \SI{95}{\percent}
confidence intervals $1.96\,s/\sqrt{n}$. All computation is CPU-only (NumPy, SciPy, scikit-learn, NetworkX,
Matplotlib). The full-scale run is launched with \texttt{scripts/run.py} on \texttt{configs/full.yaml};
\texttt{results/summary.json} is the single source of truth for every table, figure, and number, and
Tables~\ref{tab:main} and \ref{tab:extended} are generated verbatim from it by
\texttt{scripts/make\_tables.py}. The provenance block records seed, platform, library versions, runtime
(\SI{32.0}{\second}), and peak memory (\SI{621.2}{\mega\byte}).

\section{From-scratch derivation of the implemented method}
\label{sec:scratch}
This appendix is written to be read top-to-bottom by someone who has seen linear algebra and basic
probability but not single-cell biology, graph-based semi-supervised learning, or operator theory. Each
part names the source file it maps to in the package \texttt{topocell}, so that theory and implementation
can be read side by side.

\paragraph{The problem and the data (\texttt{src/topocell/synthetic.py}).}
A single-cell experiment measures thousands of cells, each a feature vector (after dimensionality
reduction). Cells lie near a low-dimensional manifold tracing differentiation. The task is forecasting:
predict the discrete state of a cell under a condition not directly observed---a donor, batch, later
timepoint, or perturbation. Only a handful of cells are annotated, and the decisive states are often rare.
We do not ship real data (\texttt{ingest.py} holds honest hooks); instead we generate a fully controlled
branching lineage with three branches over a pseudotime coordinate $t\in[0,1]$, a rare state carved from
the late tip of one fate, and donor/batch/perturbation metadata assigned independently of biology so that
the held-out splits are nontrivial.

\paragraph{From points to a directed operator (\texttt{graph.py}, \texttt{operators.py}).}
The $k$NN graph connects each cell to its $k$ nearest neighbors (either-direction symmetrization), giving a
symmetric adjacency $A$. The degree is $d_i=\sum_jA_{ij}$ and $D=\operatorname{diag}(d_i)$. The standard
smoother uses the symmetric normalized adjacency $S=D^{-1/2}AD^{-1/2}$, which is self-adjoint and therefore
blind to direction. Our operator instead reweights each directed edge by a bounded forward kernel of the
pseudotime increment, $W_{ij}=A_{ij}\exp(\beta\tanh((t_j-t_i)/\tau))$, and row-normalizes to a transition
matrix $P=D_W^{-1}W$. Because forward and backward edges receive different weights, $P$ is non-self-adjoint:
$PP^\top\neq P^\top P$. The relative non-normality $\nu(P)=\|PP^\top-P^\top P\|_F/\|P\|_F^2$ is the scalar
witness of this noncommutativity (Theorem~\ref{thm:noncomm}).

\paragraph{Spectral truncation (\texttt{operators.py}).}
The graph-Fourier modes are the eigenvectors $u_1,\dots,u_n$ of $S$; the $r$ with the largest eigenvalues
are the smoothest functions on the graph. Restricting the diffusion to their span $\Pi_r=U_rU_r^\top$ is a
spectral truncation: it keeps the low-frequency, label-relevant structure and discards high-frequency
noise, and by Theorem~\ref{thm:trunc} it gives the Eckart--Young-optimal rank-$r$ approximation of the
positive-semidefinite diffusion filter the method applies (and maximizes the retained smoothing energy
$\operatorname{Tr}(\Pi_r S)$). The truncated, lazy directed transport is
$B=U_r^\top((1-\varepsilon)I+\varepsilon P)U_r$, an $r\times r$ matrix. Truncation also makes the operator
algebra noncommutative: the truncated multiplication operators
$M_\phi^{(r)}=U_r^\top\operatorname{diag}(\phi)U_r$ do not commute (Theorem~\ref{thm:noncomm}), the graph
instance of the spectral-truncation noncommutativity of $C^*$-algebraic kernel machines.

\paragraph{The closed-form diffusion and its inductive extension (\texttt{operators.py}).}
We seed one-hot labels $F_0$ on the annotated cells and solve the reduced normal equations
$(I-\alpha B)c=(1-\alpha)U_r^\top F_0$ in closed form; the field is $F^\star=U_rc$. By
Theorem~\ref{thm:fixedpoint} this is the unique fixed point of the band-limited directed diffusion and the
limit of its power iteration, well posed for $\alpha\,\sigma_{\max}(B)<1$, and invariant to the sign gauge
of the eigenvectors. The transductive forecaster builds $A,U_r,P$ over all cells; the inductive forecaster
builds them on the training cells only and labels a held-out cell by a Gaussian-weighted average of the
training field over its nearest \emph{training} cells---a Nystr\"om/Nadaraya--Watson extension that never
touches held-out connectivity (Theorem~\ref{thm:inductive}). This is the matched inductive control that
resolves the transductive-versus-inductive confound of symmetric-smoothing studies.

\paragraph{Conformal calibration and persistent homology (\texttt{conformal.py}, \texttt{persistence.py}).}
Split conformal prediction holds out a calibration slice of the annotated cells, computes the nonconformity
scores $1-p_y$, and thresholds at the conformal quantile to form prediction sets with finite-sample
coverage $\ge1-\delta$ under exchangeability (Theorem~\ref{thm:conformal}); temperature scaling
additionally recalibrates the scalar confidence. The degree-$0$ persistence barcode of the cell cloud is
computed exactly by union--find on the $k$NN distance graph---the finite $H_0$ bars are the
minimum-spanning-tree edge weights---so $\beta_0(\epsilon)=\#\{\text{bars}>\epsilon\}+1$ is a genuine
multiscale invariant, and the per-cell $k$-th-nearest-neighbor distance is the per-cell witness of a long
bar, used by the active sampler.

\paragraph{Reproducibility object (\texttt{runner.py}, \texttt{scripts/}).}
\texttt{runner.py} ties everything together: it generates the lineage, builds the operator, records the
non-normality and commutator diagnostics and the persistence barcode, runs the four forecasters under all
four splits over five seeds, calibrates by conformal prediction, compares active-sampling policies, and
writes \texttt{results/summary.json}---the single source of truth. Nothing in the paper is typed by hand:
\texttt{make\_tables.py} renders Tables~\ref{tab:main} and \ref{tab:extended} and \texttt{make\_figures.py}
renders Figs.~\ref{fig:schematic}--\ref{fig:calibration} from that file. To reproduce everything from the
\texttt{code/} folder:
\begin{verbatim}
export KMP_DUPLICATE_LIB_OK=TRUE
pip install .
python scripts/run.py \
    --config configs/full.yaml \
    --out results
python scripts/make_tables.py
python scripts/make_figures.py
\end{verbatim}
The reported run uses $\num{3000}$ cells, $\num{16}$ features, $\num{8}$ donors, $\num{4}$ batches,
$\num{5}$ perturbations, $\sim$\SI{4}{\percent} rare state, $k=\num{20}$, truncation rank $r=\num{80}$,
$\alpha=0.9$, $\beta=1.0$, $\varepsilon=0.6$, and $\num{5}$ seeds; the provenance block records a runtime of
\SI{32.0}{\second} and peak memory of \SI{621.2}{\mega\byte} on a laptop CPU.
