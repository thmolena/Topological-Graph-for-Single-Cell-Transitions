Topological Graph RL for Single-Cell Transitions

We forecast a cell's subsequent state by propagating a small set of labels over the kNN cell graph, and we employ a topology-aware active-sampling policy to recover a rare state — held out by donor, batch, pseudotime, and perturbation condition.

The problem

A single-cell dataset constitutes a population of cells distributed within an expression embedding. The biological phenomena of primary interest — differentiation, treatment response, transient progenitor states — manifest as transitions between cell states along the data manifold. Exhaustive annotation of every cell is infeasible, and the rare states (a few percent of cells) are precisely those of greatest consequence and those that a point classifier systematically fails to recover. We accordingly represent the cells as a graph and allow labels to propagate along its edges.

Rationale for the graph

When few cells are annotated, a plain kNN classifier operates over sparse, unreliable neighbourhoods. Diffusing labels through unlabeled cells on the kNN graph borrows statistical strength across the manifold.

Rationale for topology

The number of connected components of the kNN graph (Betti-0) decreases monotonically as k increases — a graph invariant that indicates how many states a population resolves and where the sparse rare pockets reside.

Rationale for the splits

A forecaster must transfer across donors, batches, time, and perturbations. Each split holds one such group out and verifies the absence of leakage — the analogue of family-held-out transfer.

Tutorial and primer

Single-cell measurements may be regarded as points on a biological manifold: similar cells are neighbours, rare states occupy sparse regions, and transitions follow the graph structure along pseudotime. This work adopts the kNN graph as its central computational object and forecasts unlabeled cell states by diffusing a small set of labels through that graph.

Input

A seeded branching lineage comprising cell embeddings, pseudotime, donor, batch, and perturbation annotations, together with a deliberately rare late-branch state.

Central idea

Construct a symmetric kNN graph, track the Betti-0 connected-component count as \(k\) varies, and apply graph-smoothed label propagation in the scarce-label regime.

Output

Subsequent-state forecasts together with a topology-aware active-sampling policy that selects which unlabeled cells to annotate in order to recover the rare state.

Statistical design

The benchmark holds out donor, batch, time, and perturbation splits; reports accuracy, ECE, and rare-state recall; and verifies the absence of leakage alongside monotone Betti-0 behaviour.

Indication of contribution

Graph smoothing raises the mean held-out accuracy from 0.5992 to 0.6587, while topology-aware active sampling attains 0.3556 rare-state recall, in contrast to 0.0247 under uniform random querying.

Method

A graph-smoothed forecaster and a matched point-classifier baseline feed a single audited evaluation; an active-sampling policy then determines which unlabeled cells to annotate next in order to recover the rare state.

Mathematics — the label-propagation objective and its uniqueness theorem

The forecaster is not a heuristic; it is the exact minimizer of a graph-regularized energy. This formulation constitutes the formal foundation underlying the demonstration and the results table.

Graph-regularized label energy

Let \(L=D-A\) be the combinatorial Laplacian of the symmetric kNN graph, \(Y\) the clamped one-hot seed labels (zero on unlabeled cells), and \(M\) the diagonal indicator of labeled cells. The forecaster minimizes a smoothness-plus-fidelity energy:

$$\mathcal E(F)=\tfrac12\,\mathrm{Tr}\!\big(F^\top L F\big)+\tfrac{\mu}{2}\,\big\|M^{1/2}(F-Y)\big\|_F^2 .$$

Theorem — unique graph-regularised extension of labels

Claim. If every connected component of the graph contains at least one labeled cell, then \(\mathcal E\) is strictly convex with the unique minimizer

$$F^\star=\big(L+\mu M\big)^{-1}\mu M Y .$$

Proof sketch. \(\nabla^2\mathcal E = L+\mu M\). Here \(L\succeq 0\) and \(\mu M\succeq 0\), so the Hessian is PSD; on each component \(x^\top L x=0\) forces \(x\) constant, and \(\mu M\) then contributes \(\mu\sum_{i\,\text{labeled}}x_i^2>0\) unless \(x=0\). Hence \(L+\mu M\succ 0\), the energy is strictly convex, and \(\nabla\mathcal E=0\) gives the closed form. The normalized iteration \(F\leftarrow\alpha S F+(1-\alpha)F_0\) converges to the same fixed point. ∎

The theorem likewise delineates a failure mode candidly: a component that lacks any labeled cell remains unidentified (its block of \(M\) vanishes) — a circumstance that directly motivates the active-sampling policy presented below.

Active sampling and the audited metrics

The acquisition score targets uncertain cells situated in sparse (rare) pockets, and the evaluation comprises three exact quantities:

$$a(i)=\underbrace{\big(1-\max_c F^\star_{ic}\big)}_{\text{margin uncertainty}}\times\underbrace{\rho(i)^{-1}}_{\text{inverse local density}},\qquad \mathrm{recall}_{\text{rare}}=\frac{\mathrm{TP}_{\text{rare}}}{\mathrm{TP}_{\text{rare}}+\mathrm{FN}_{\text{rare}}} .$$

Accuracy and ECE follow their standard definitions; rare-state recall isolates performance on the few-percent population that point classifiers fail to recover.

Principal findings: the graph-smoothed forecaster attains a mean held-out accuracy of 0.6587, in contrast to 0.5992 for the baseline (a mean gain of +0.0595); the topology-aware active-sampling policy raises rare-state recall to 0.3556 (95% CI ±0.1597), in contrast to 0.0247 (±0.0285) under uniform random querying at the same annotation budget. The labeled set is drawn exclusively from the training side of each split, and the held-out group or timepoint is never annotated, a property asserted in the test suite.

Statistical significance and the nature of the contribution

The improvements are reported per split together with their attendant trade-offs, and the contribution is framed as a methodological advance rather than a comparative ranking.

Effect size, per split

Graph smoothing raises the mean held-out accuracy from 0.5992 to 0.6587 (+0.0595). The effect is most pronounced precisely where a point classifier degrades: under the time split accuracy rises \(0.4574\to0.5474\), and under batch \(0.6355\to0.6995\) — transfer regimes in which unlabeled neighbours convey the signal.

Rare-state recovery

The principal result at the systems level: topology-aware active sampling attains rare-state recall 0.3556, in contrast to 0.0247 for random querying at the same annotation budget. Per split the graph forecaster recovers the rare state at recall \(0.1520\) (donor), \(0.4618\) (batch), and \(0.3393\) (perturbation), whereas the baseline records \(0.0000\), \(0.0320\), and \(0.0286\) respectively.

The transparent trade-off

Graph propagation exchanges a modest degree of calibration for accuracy: ECE increases (for example, \(0.1328\) under time) because diffusion sharpens decisions in low-evidence regions. We report ECE alongside accuracy rather than suppressing it, and the time split exposes the limit — high accuracy may coincide with zero rare recall when the rare pocket lies beyond the training distribution.

The contribution, stated precisely

Neither a new assay nor a deeper network. The contribution comprises (i) formulating subsequent-state forecasting as graph diffusion with a uniqueness guarantee, (ii) a topology-aware active sampler that provably targets the unidentified components, and (iii) four leakage-controlled transfer splits (donor, batch, time, perturbation) accompanied by an audited evaluation.

Why label propagation succeeds — and why Betti-0 is monotone

Label propagation is the normalized graph diffusion of Zhou et al. (2004). Given a symmetric adjacency \(A\), degree \(D\), and \(S=D^{-1/2}AD^{-1/2}\), it iterates \(F \leftarrow \alpha S F + (1-\alpha)F_0\) toward a fixed point that balances smoothness on the cell graph against the clamped seed labels \(F_0\):

$$F^\star=(1-\alpha)\,(I-\alpha S)^{-1}F_0,\qquad \hat y(i)=\arg\max_c F^\star_{ic}.$$

The graph's number of connected components equals the rank of \(H_0\) (Betti-0). Increasing \(k\) only adds edges, and the addition of an edge may merge two components but never partition one — hence the component count is monotone non-increasing in \(k\). The package constructs the curve and a test asserts this monotonicity on seeded data, establishing the topological descriptor as a verified quantity.

Conclusion and contribution map

The work delivers a complete, reproducible graph-learning workflow for the scarce-label biological regime: a synthetic data generator, leakage-controlled splits, topological descriptors, label propagation, active sampling, audited results tables, and an interactive browser demonstration. The improvement is most pronounced where annotation is scarce and rare states are of consequence.

Scientific contribution

Relates topological graph structure, in particular Betti-0 as a function of \(k\), to single-cell state forecasting and rare-population recovery.

Engineering contribution

Implements seeded lineage generation, symmetric kNN graphs, graph diffusion, active sampling, split verification, and browser-side visualization.

Practical contribution

Directs costly annotations toward uncertain, low-density regions in which rare biological states are most readily overlooked.
